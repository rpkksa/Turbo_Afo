What is Foot Drop?

Foot drop, also referred to as drop foot, is the inability to lift the foot, especially the front part of your foot (forefoot). This typically results in dragging the foot on the ground during walking. Foot drop is not a disease but a sign of underlying pathology, that produces weakness or paralysis of the muscles associated with raising the foot (dorsiflexion). It may strike one foot (unilateral) or both feet (bilateral). This may provoke unsteadiness in walking with frequent falls.

How to treat footdrop?

Treatment and its outcome usually revolve around treating the underlying cause. If there is severe damage to the extent where the pathology cannot be reverted, it may lead to a permanent foot drop. The common management strategy is Ankle-foot orthotics

Ankle-foot orthotics

It's the most common and safe way of treating foot drop. An ankle-foot orthosis (AFO) is a hard brace worn on the lower leg that improves overall walking safety and efficiency for people with certain medical conditions. AFOs provide gait stability, keep joints properly aligned, and help compensate for muscle weakness.
